# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary audience is the product owner reviewing a working Polymarket trading demo. They need to understand the account state, wallet setup, deposit wallet readiness, balances, market discovery, and order flow without reading implementation notes or asking an engineer to explain each screen.

## Product Purpose

This app demonstrates a Privy-authenticated Polymarket trading workflow using deposit wallets, builder credentials, and CLOB/relayer integrations. Success means the demo feels like a real product workbench: clear status, clear next action, trustworthy trading controls, and visible places for future product decisions.

## Brand Personality

Clear, controlled, credible.

The interface should feel like a trading workbench: calm enough for review, structured enough for operations, and polished enough to show product maturity.

## Anti-references

Do not make it feel like a decorative Web3 landing page, a crypto casino, or a terminal for high-frequency traders. Avoid gratuitous neon, glassmorphism as the default, animated spectacle, vague wallet jargon, and dense controls without state explanation.

## Design Principles

1. Make state legible before action: connection, session, wallet, balances, region access, and readiness should be visible at a glance.
2. Keep trading controls calm: buy/sell and risk colors are semantic, not decorative.
3. Use familiar product patterns: standard buttons, tabs, dialogs, tooltips, inputs, and focus behavior should feel predictable.
4. Preserve demo clarity: show enough guidance for a product owner to follow the workflow without turning the app into a tutorial.
5. Build a reusable component layer before adding flourish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls in both light and dark themes. Support keyboard navigation for dialogs, tabs, buttons, and theme controls. Respect reduced motion preferences; motion should communicate state only.
